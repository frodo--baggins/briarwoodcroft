In 1977 we purchased ten acres of land, which included a wooded lot and pasture. The first animals we purchased for our farm were two Corriedale ewes. Shortly thereafter, we acquired a pair of La Mancha goats. For many years, the sheep provided us with lamb meat and the goats provided us with goat milk. The goat milk was especially important because our younger son and I were allergic to cow's milk. As the years went on, we added a flock of layer hens and purchased a Gloustershire Old Spots sow and boar.

When we began raising sheep, we could not find organic feed at any of the feed mills near us. Therefore, we purchased feed that was composed only of locally grown grains. As time went on, we found a local farmer who began to sell organic, non-GMO animal feed. Currently, all of our animals are raised on organic, non-GMO grain and organic pasture.

We are not certified organic, but we follow organic practices, which include using untreated locust posts for our fencing.
